How to Create a Book for a Young Person

First, think about the messages you wish to incorporate. The more you find, the better. We will use them as a pool to choose from, as it is possible they cannot all be used in the same story. What could these messages be?

  • Tell about your family's cultural heritage through facts and events.
  • Tell about your family's personal heritage and identity through facts and events.
  • Tell about your personal life and the major events that shaped it.
  • Think about what your hopes for the future are and how your child/grandchild is a part of them.
  • Think about the warnings you would like to convey or about the pitfalls you would them to avoid. Imagine scenarios you would want/not want them to be part of.
  • Think about stories from the past, details about the family tree, lost places and people, memories and hopes.
  • Think about your child's plans for the future and how you hope you might fit into them.

Then, let us know about these ideas. They don't have to be very coherent, or very specific. However, if you do have a story in mind, or a better developed set of ideas, don't hesitate to send it over. Use our 'Introduction' form and fill in the text field. Inputs are scanned automatically before they reach an editor - be sure they are free of profanity or topics that we specifically do not address (see the Terms and Conditions).
